- Decision: ITERATE
- Justification: The current project is at a critical juncture where the "Lévy-equivalence" mapping is yielding results that are physically inconsistent (e.g., the Lévy walk ground truth itself shows systematic underestimation of $\alpha$ and non-Gaussian increments). Simply reporting these as "model failures" would result in a weak paper. However, the data (point-vortex tracers) contains rich, high-fidelity Hamiltonian dynamics that are currently being under-utilized. By shifting the focus from "forcing a fit" to "characterizing the breakdown of the Lévy model," the paper gains significant scientific depth. Implementing the proposed changes—specifically the residence time distribution and the VACF analysis—will transform the manuscript from a descriptive curve-fitting exercise into a mechanistic study of Hamiltonian chaos. This shift directly addresses the "So What?" factor by providing a physical explanation for the observed transport regimes, thereby moving the manuscript from a "Borderline" to a "Strong" contribution by providing a nuanced, physics-based critique of the standard Lévy-walk paradigm. The ROI is high because the necessary data is already present in the existing simulations; the effort is purely analytical.
