**Title: Finite-Time Scaling and Lévy-Stable Mapping in Chaotic Vortex Flows**

We derive the velocity PDF of passive tracers from the Kirchhoff-Onsager Hamiltonian, demonstrating that the $1/r$ vortex kernel induces heavy-tailed statistics governed by the vortex density $n$. We model the tracer transport as a finite-time stochastic process, using the `levy_walk_trajectories.npy` dataset to establish a Bayesian-consistent confidence interval for the anomalous exponent $\alpha$ and tail index $\mu$ that accounts for the $0.02$ m noise floor. By calculating the Time-Averaged Mean Squared Displacement (TAMSD) for $N \in \{5, 10, 20, 40\}$ configurations, we identify the crossover time where tracer dynamics transition from local trapping to Lévy-like superdiffusion. We compare the empirical $\alpha(N)$ scaling against the Lévy walk ground truth to quantify the effective "Lévy-equivalence" of point-vortex systems. The final output is a validated state-space mapping that predicts the vortex density $n$ from the observed tracer MSD slope and velocity increment tails, providing a robust theoretical link between Hamiltonian chaoticity and anomalous transport.
