The failure of the velocity increment PDF analysis for the Lévy walk dataset is directly attributable to the dataset's construction. The description specifies that the Lévy walk trajectories are observed at discrete time intervals ($dt = 0.1$ s) and that the minimum flight time is equal to $dt$. Consequently, single-step velocity increments are bounded by the maximum displacement per step ($v_0 \times dt = 0.1$ m), which prevents the emergence of the power-law tails characteristic of the underlying Lévy-stable process. This constraint explains why the Hill estimator and Lévy-stable MLE failed to recover the theoretical tail indices for the ground truth data.

Additionally, the limited sample size (5 tracers per point-vortex configuration and 10 trajectories per Lévy walk class) is responsible for the high variance and wide confidence intervals observed in the TAMSD and ergodicity-breaking analyses. This constraint limits the ability to distinguish between transient ballistic behavior and true asymptotic superdiffusion, as evidenced by the unreliable TAMSD-based estimates for the Lévy walk classes.
