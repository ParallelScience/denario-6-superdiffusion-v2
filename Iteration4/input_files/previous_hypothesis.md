The "stickiness" and non-ergodicity observed in point-vortex tracers, which deviate from pure Lévy walks, are driven by the spatial persistence of vortex-induced velocity structures (coherent Lagrangian structures). I hypothesize that the tracer dynamics are governed by a "Tempered Lévy-Stable" process, where the heavy-tailed flight distribution is truncated by the finite spatial correlation length of the vortex field. By calculating the spatial correlation function of the velocity field $C(r) = \langle v(x)v(x+r) \rangle$ and using it to define a spatially-dependent tempering parameter $\lambda(N)$, we can map the point-vortex system to a Tempered Lévy Walk model. This model will resolve the discrepancy between the observed EB profiles and the pure Lévy walk ground truth, providing a superior fit for the MSD scaling by accounting for the transition from ballistic to superdiffusive to diffusive regimes as a function of the vortex density $N$.
